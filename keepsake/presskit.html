Background

The Story

Every parent has the pile. The shoebox in the wardrobe, the drawer in the kitchen that's too full to close, the shelf that holds a child's first painting and a birthday card from a grandmother who is no longer here. These things matter, but most of them will eventually be lost to a house move, a flood, or time.

Keepsake started from a simple frustration: there was no good way to preserve these objects digitally without reducing them to files in a camera roll.

"These aren't just photos. They're the things that will matter in twenty years. I wanted an app that treated them that way."

Keepsake is built around revisiting, not just storing. Memories resurface quietly over time through optional reminders designed to feel thoughtful rather than demanding.

Keepsake is built specifically for Apple devices, using native technologies for privacy, spatial capture, and AR. No cross-platform compromises. No external servers. Your memories live in your iCloud account and nowhere else.

Keepsake was built solo by Harry Brown of Chaotic Good Creations, a design and development studio based in New Zealand. It is the studio's most personal project. A product built because Harry needed it, and because he believes the technology now exists to do it properly.

Key Features

What Keepsake Does

📷

Smart Capture

VisionKit document scanning for artwork, cards, and letters. Full 3D photogrammetry for physical objects via LiDAR. Guided capture flows for every keepsake type.

🗂️

Memory Boxes

Organise keepsakes into colour-coded boxes. Isla's Art, Gran's Cards, School Year Three. A visual, physical metaphor for how memories actually live in our homes.

🧊

3D Model Viewing

Physical keepsakes can be captured as interactive 3D models that users can rotate, inspect, and revisit from every angle.

🔮

AR Experience

Place any keepsake in the real world using ARKit. Lay artwork flat on a table, stand a card upright on a shelf, or see a 3D object floating in your living room. Premium feature.

🔒

Genuinely Private

No account. No email. No data sold, no ads. Everything syncs exclusively to your personal iCloud account via CloudKit. Keepsake has no access to your memories.

🔔

Gentle Reminders

A weekly invitation to revisit something meaningful. A personalised 7-day look-back after each new keepsake is saved. Notifications that feel like a tap on the shoulder, not a badge count.

📝

Rich Metadata

Capture who made it, how old they were, when it was made, and the story behind it. A keepsake without context is just a scan; with context it becomes a memory.

☁️

iCloud Sync

Captures on one device appear on all your devices, automatically. Private CloudKit database, the same infrastructure Apple uses for Photos, Health, and Messages.

Frequently Asked

Common Questions

Who is Keepsake built for?
Primarily parents, grandparents, and teachers who accumulate meaningful physical objects — children's artwork, birthday cards, handwritten letters — and worry about losing them. Also people who inherit objects with sentimental value and want to preserve them with context and story, not just as photographs.
How is this different from just taking a photo?
Keepsake preserves context, not just images — who made it, how old they were, when, and the story behind it. It treats the capture as a deliberate act of preservation, not a casual snapshot. For physical objects, it builds a navigable 3D model rather than a flat photo. And it invites you back to memories over time via gentle reminders, rather than letting them disappear into a camera roll of thousands.
Is this available on Android or iPad?
V1 is iPhone-only. The app is built on native Apple technologies (SwiftUI, RealityKit, ARKit, Object Capture) that are exclusive to the Apple ecosystem. Android is not planned. Support for other Apple devices will come in a future release.
How does the 3D capture work? What hardware is required?
3D object capture uses Apple's Object Capture API (photogrammetry), which requires a LiDAR scanner. This is available on iPhone 12 Pro and later Pro and Pro Max models. All other capture types — artwork, cards, letters — use VisionKit and work on any iPhone capable of running iOS 26.
Can I see or access user keepsakes?
No — technically and by design. All keepsake data is stored exclusively in the user's personal iCloud private database using CloudKit. Chaotic Good Creations has no access to user keepsakes, no server where they are stored, and no visibility into the content. This is not a marketing claim; it is a structural property of the architecture.
Do you use AI or machine learning?
The 3D model building uses Apple's PhotogrammetrySession, which is Apple's computer vision framework that runs entirely on-device. There is no external AI API, no content sent to a language model, and no generative features. The capture and organisation experience is intentionally human-directed.
How does Keepsake make money?
Through a freemium subscription model via the App Store. Premium unlocks unlimited keepsakes, unlimited memory boxes, and the full AR experience. There are no ads, no data monetisation, and no dark patterns.
